Who We Are

We are a group of researchers who examine the aesthetics and politics of media by blending media theory and history with media practice:

  • Tracy El Hajj (English)
  • Julie Funk (English and CSPT)
  • Stefan Higgins (English and CSPT)
  • Hector Lopez (English)
  • Faith Ryan (English)
  • Jentery Sayers (English and CSPT; director)

We value prototyping, situated knowledge, the space of place, attention to social justice and poiesis, and integrative approaches to media: old + new, analog + digital, paper + electronic, popular + experimental, immersive + meta.

We respectfully acknowledge that our studio is located on the unceded territory of the Lkwungen peoples and the Songhees, Esquimalt, and W̱SÁNEĆ First Nations, whose ongoing historical relationships with the land continue to this day.

What We Do

Each year we identify several pressing topics in media studies, research them, prototype scenarios for them, write about them, and facilitate UVic events to engage them. Our topics for 2019-20 are:

  • Building Illiberal Worlds: How to imagine worlds and roleplay without reproducing legacies of dispossesion and inequality?
  • Life with Artificial Intelligence: How is AI entwined with health and creativity? How are people studying those relationships?
  • Conversations with Canned Sound: How to talk and learn with online audio archives beyond the "digital assistant" paradigm?

If you want to learn more about this research, then please don't hesitate to contact us or attend one of our events.

What We Aren't

We are not a blog, brand, dev team, fab lab, or digital humanities centre. We do not offer courses, either, but if you're at UVic and looking to learn more about digital tools and methodologies, then we recommend contacting our amazing colleagues at the Digital Scholarship Commons (DSC) and the Humanities Computing and Media Centre (HCMC).

Support and History

We are housed in the Faculty of Humanities; we partner with English, Visual Arts, the HCMC, and Cultural, Social, and Political Thought; and we collaborate with the DSC and Special Collections at UVic. We are members of the SpokenWeb project, and our research is supported by the Social Sciences and Humanities Research Council of Canada (SSHRC) and the Canada Foundation for Innovation (CFI). We began as The Maker Lab in the Humanities in 2012 and became The Praxis Studio for Comparative Media Studies in 2019. All work belongs to our researchers, not to the studio.
